NEERAJ CHOPRA - JAVELIN THROWER


NEERAJ CHOPRA

Know More Bio graphy

Neeraj Chopra is an incredibly talented Indian javelin thrower who made history by winning India's first-ever Olympic gold medal in athletics at the Tokyo 2020 Olympics. Born on December 24, 1997, in Panipat, Haryana, he showed promise in javelin throw from a young age. Neeraj has broken several records in his career and has been a consistent performer in international competitions.

His breakthrough moment came at the 2018 Commonwealth Games where he won gold with a throw of 86.47 meters. He continued his success by clinching gold at the 2018 Asian Games, setting a national record with a throw of 88.06 meters.Born on December 24, 1997, in Panipat, Haryana, he showed promise in javelin throw from a young age. Neeraj has broken several records in his career and has been a consistent performer in international competitions.At the Tokyo Olympics in 2020, Neeraj Chopra won the gold medal with a throw of 87.58 meters in the men's javelin event, becoming India's first track and field Olympic champion. His achievement was historic and monumental for Indian athletics, making him a national hero and inspiring countless aspiring athletes in the country. Neeraj Chopra's dedication, hard work, and talent have made him a role model for many, and his achievements have brought immense pride to the nation.

Absolutely, Neeraj Chopra's journey to becoming an Olympic champion is quite remarkable. His success story is a testament to his dedication, hard work, and passion for javelin throw. Neeraj's interest in sports, particularly athletics, was sparked during his school days. He initially pursued cricket but later shifted his focus to javelin throw after being inspired by a senior's success in the sport. His talent was quickly recognized, and under the guidance of coach Jaiveer, Neeraj honed his skills in javelin throw. He began participating in various national and international competitions, steadily making a mark in the sport. Neeraj Chopra's breakthrough on the international stage came at the 2016 World U20 Championships where he won the gold medal with a junior world record throw of 86.48 meters. This victory catapulted him into the limelight, and he continued to impress in subsequent competitions. His achievements include gold medals at prestigious events like the Asian Athletics Championships and the Commonwealth Games. Neeraj's consistency and exceptional performances led to high expectations as he headed to the Tokyo 2020 Olympics.
